FAQ

* What is BMI?

BMI stands for Body Mass Index. It is a numerical value of a person's weight in relation to their height. BMI is commonly used as an indicator of body fatness and a screening tool for health-related issues.

* How is BMI calculated?

BMI is calculated using the formula: BMI = weight (kg) / (height (m))^2. If weight is in pounds and height is in inches, the formula is modified to BMI = (weight (lb) / (height (in))^2) x 703.

* Can BMI be used for children?

BMI-for-age is used for children, as the normal BMI range varies with age and gender. It is interpreted differently for children and is compared to growth charts.

* Can I use this BMI calculator for fitness assessments?

While BMI is a useful health screening tool, it may not be the best indicator for fitness. Fitness assessments often involve additional measurements and tests beyond BMI.

* How accurate is BMI as a measure of health?

While BMI is a useful screening tool, it doesn't directly measure body fat percentage or distribution. It may not be accurate for individuals with high muscle mass or other factors affecting body composition. It's best used in conjunction with other health assessments.
